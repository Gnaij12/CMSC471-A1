  1. Dataset: We used the Daily Weather in the U.S., 2017 Dataset. This dataset contains weather data from all of the United States, including the territories. It contains temperatures, winds, and precipitation data. However, we decided to take a subset of the data by only using the 50 states and only using temperature from those states. We use minimum temperature, maximum temperature, and our own temperature range variable to display. We also use the date, the state, and the longitude/latitude for making our visualization.

  2. Rationale for our design decisions: Because our weather data has longitude and latitude coordinates, we thought it would be more interesting and understandable to use a map. Therefore, we displayed a US map and mapped the coordinates onto that. Then, we wanted a way to easily show temperature differences, so we used both size and color to do so. However, with the natural differences in temperatures accross different months and days, we wanted to allow users to check specific days with a slider. \ We figured the slider would be the easiest and most intuitive method to do this.
